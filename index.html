Who am I

Healthcare background

I graduated as a physiotherapist in 2015 in a small island of the Atlantic Ocean and just one year after that I took my chances and moved to London in search for better professional and personal development opportunities.

Thanks to stepping outside my comfort zone, I started to see the world differently. Meeting people from all walks of life and learning from improved my communication skills greatly.

This experience also motivated me to keep learning different things, both related and unrelated to my working environment.

Little did I know that I would be able to make better use of my skills in a different setting.

My passion

As I have always been into learning and challenging my brain, I would find myself playing puzzle games and studying different things by myself on my spare time. I learnt some languages, I learnt to play basic piano songs, I learnt new recipes, you name it.

So when I found FreeCodeCamp and realized I loved coding, I started to code as often as I could. The joy of solving a problem I had been stuck for a while is something out of this world, I had to save money to be able to stop working and study full time, so I could make it my profession.

And so, I finished my last placement in December 2019 to started working on projects and attending Hackatons and other coding meetups to better direct my learning and create my own projects.

My hobbies

As a learning addict, I must say that I find great pleasure in reading, problem solving and watching documentaries. I often find myself just testing or playing random puzzle or logic games online just for the sake of challenging myself (and now that I have been attending to hackatons I have endless ways of doing so in codewars).

On the other hand, I am also very active, loving sports like hiking, hockey, yoga, surfing and bouldering (although I have a hate-love relationship with this last one, I will overcome my fears eventually).

However, in terms of growing and learning, I can say that traveling is one of the things that have helped me the most.
